Hello there, I’m

Pradyut Kumar.

but most people call me Prads

I’m a PhD student in the Ecology & Evolutionary Biology division at Purdue Biology, where I get to think about disease spread, drug resistance, and math (on purpose!). I’m fortunate to be advised by Dr. Qixin He and broadly interested in using mathematical models to make sense of messy biological phenomena — from predicting disease outbreaks to designing better intervention strategies (yes, math can save lives).

Before crossing continents to Indiana, I completed a dual degree at BITS Pilani — an M.Sc. in Biological Sciences and a B.E. in Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering.

When I’m not debugging models or defending math in rooms full of biologists, you can probably find me overthinking something I read, scribbling sketches of coffee cups, or making bad puns to be kept to myself.
